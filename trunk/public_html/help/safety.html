Communication & Safety Tips

Response to book inquiry:

When you respond to an inquiry about your book be careful about disclosing personal information, use good judgment. An adequate reply:

Dear Bob481,

I still have the book you are interested in purchasing. If you are willing to pay the ask price, let me know a time and place you would like to meet. If you feel comfortable we could exchange phone numbers or instant messenger information and discuss the details further.

   Sincerely,
     Kyle9012

Things to discuss:

  1. Price and/or trade details
  2. Confirm condition of book
  3. Ask if seller would be willing to give you any of their previous study materials from the class to help you
  4. Specific location and time (Mountainlair, next to the Mountaineer Statue, 11:30 am)
  5. Have book in hand
  6. Be on time, if running late, try to contact seller/buyer
  7. What you look like and will be wearing

The Meeting:

A first meeting with any new person can be awkward, and most first meetings are perfectly safe. But it's always smart to take basic precautions. Always trust your instincts. Be sure when meeting to make a transaction (trade, purchase, sale) on campus follow these guidelines:

  1. DO meet in public.
    • ALWAYS arrange to meet in a populated, well lit, public place. NEVER meet in a private or remote location.
  2. DO tell a friend.
    • Tell at least one friend or family member you are meeting, where you are going and when you expect to return, or have a friend come with you.
      • Tip: Contact your friend before and after or ask your friend to contact you at a predetermined time.
  3. DON'T leave home without your mobile phone.
